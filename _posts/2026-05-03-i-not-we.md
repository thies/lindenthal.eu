For a while now, I have been using Claude Code and other AI models intensively. They are exciting tools and have changed the way I work and write. In academic writing, it is uncommon to say “I” — the convention is “we”, even for sole-authored papers. In the age of AI-enabled research, I think we need to change that.

If the text says *I did this... I tested that... I find...*, it makes absolutely clear who is responsible. The author, alone. No *we*, no *Claude wrote this*. It is my text. When I claim authorship, I also own all errors.
